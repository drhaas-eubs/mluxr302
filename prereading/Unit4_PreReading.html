Key Framework: Kering Environmental Profit & Loss (EP&L)

Can Luxury and Sustainability Co-Exist?

Pre-Reading · Unit 4 · Sustainability Performance of Leading Fashion & Luxury Brands

Overview

Before benchmarking brand sustainability performance in Unit 4, understanding how environmental profit and loss (EP&L) accounting works — and why Kering developed it — establishes the methodological foundation for comparing brands across different business models. EP&L is arguably the most rigorous attempt to date to monetise the external costs of fashion production.

The Framework: Kering Environmental Profit & Loss (EP&L)

Kering's EP&L methodology assigns monetary values to six categories of environmental impact: greenhouse gas emissions, land use, water consumption, water pollution, air pollution and waste production. The result is a single number — in euros — representing the environmental cost generated by a company's operations and supply chain. In 2022, Kering's group EP&L was €2.3 billion, meaning the company generated €2.3 billion in environmental costs not reflected in its financial accounts.

Key Data Points

90% of Kering's EP&L is generated in Tier 3 and 4 of the supply chain — raw material production — which is least visible to consumers.

Kering uses EP&L to identify where investment in sustainability generates the greatest return: reducing Tier 4 impact is 100× more cost-effective than Tier 1.

If all fashion brands accounted for their EP&L, the industry would show losses exceeding reported profits in many financial years.

Prepare for Session

Review Kering's most recent Annual Sustainability Report and identify the five highest-impact brands in their portfolio by EP&L score. What patterns do you observe in terms of product category, price point and supply chain structure?
